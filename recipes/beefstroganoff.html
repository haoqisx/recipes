Beef Stroganoff

Description

First created in Tsarist Russia, beef stroganoff is a widely renowned dish, known for its delightfully aromatic sauce and its near-addictive egg noodles. Although not the most common dish, one taste is enough to convince anyone that beef stroganoff deserves a spot among the best dishes mankind has concocted. It originally gained popularity for being a rather simple meal to cook, but that the procedure is not exceedingly complex has no ties to its flavor; in fact, many argue that no overengineered, intricate mess of a dish can replicate the deep, rich, and uniquely homey palate of a good beef stroganoff.

Ingredients

  • 1 (8 ounce) package of egg noodles
  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 (10.5 ounce) can of fat-free condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 1/2 cup of sour cream
  • Salt and ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Saut√© ground beef in a large skillet over medium heat until browned and crumbly; 5 to 10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rapid boil. Cook egg noodles at a boil until tender yet firm to the bite, 7 to 9 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  4. Drain and discard any fat from the cooked beef. Stir condensed soup and garlic powder into the beef. Simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. Remove beef from the heat. Add egg noodles and stir to combine. Stir in sour cream and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy!
